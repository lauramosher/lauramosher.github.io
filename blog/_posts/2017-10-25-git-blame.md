I was priviledged with giving a lightning talk at Windy City Rails in 2017.

This talk is a humorous take on git's `blame` tool. Often times when we look through code, we find snippets that invoke a... variety of reactions. Even though using `blame` told me what I needed to know (who did it), using `blame` rarely felt good. In fact, in a culture of feedback and mentorship, typing blame is counterproductive. So, I made a bunch of aliases to help remove the blame game.
